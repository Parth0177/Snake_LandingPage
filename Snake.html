Why Snakebite Awareness?

Snakebites are a major public health issue, especially in rural areas. Our program provides life-saving knowledge and tools to reduce deaths and disabilities caused by venomous snakes.

Common Venomous Snakes

Here are some of the most dangerous snakes that pose a threat to humans:

  • Cobra: Known for its hood, cobras can inject a potent neurotoxin, leading to paralysis or death if untreated.
  • Viper: The Russell's viper and other vipers deliver venom that causes severe tissue damage and blood clotting.
  • Krait: A small but highly venomous snake. Its bite can lead to respiratory failure.
  • Black Mamba: One of the fastest snakes, its venom can kill in under 30 minutes if not treated.
  • Coral Snake: Known for its distinctive color pattern, the venom attacks the nervous system, causing paralysis.

Prevention Tips

  • Wear boots and long pants in grassy or forested areas.
  • Never stick hands into holes, logs, or rock crevices.
  • Use a flashlight when walking at night.
  • Keep living areas and storage sheds clean to deter snakes.

Snakebite Symptoms & First Aid

  • Symptoms: Swelling, pain, nausea, blurred vision, or difficulty breathing.
  • First Aid: Stay calm, restrict movement, remove tight items, rush to hospital. Do not cut or suck the wound.
  • Antivenom: Only administered by medical professionals.
